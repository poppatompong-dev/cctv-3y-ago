# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Nakhon Sawan Municipality executives, administrative officers, and IT/cabling engineering teams responsible for planning, allocating, and approving repair budgets for the city's public CCTV security cameras.

## Product Purpose
To transition from a static infographic to a highly interactive Single Page Application (SPA) dashboard that tracks and analyzes Nakhon Sawan's 3-year CCTV repair budget (2567-2569). It enables administrators to discover core "Cost Drivers" (such as transmission lines and electrical grid repair costs), simulate budget-reduction scenarios, and evaluate data-driven preventative maintenance proposals.

## Brand Personality
- **Authoritative**: Instills financial confidence, governmental professionalism, and security engineering expertise.
- **Analytical**: Clear, clean data visualization, highlighting core patterns without unnecessary noise.
- **Forward-looking**: Promotes preventative, systemic structural improvements over reactive, repetitive quick fixes.

## Anti-references
- **SaaS Cliché**: Avoid generic flat gray/blue dashboard layouts that look like a generic tech startup.
- **Overwhelming Infographics**: Avoid bright, distracting color palettes, decorative neon glow lines, or overly dense text blocks that overwhelm government executives.
- **Static Spreadsheets**: Avoid simple plain tables with no interactive connection or simulation features.

## Design Principles
1. **Highlight the Core Cost Driver**: The dashboard must guide the user's attention naturally to the primary expense category (cabling/network, 61.3% of budget) as the chief lever for budget optimization.
2. **Context-Aware Visual Cues**: Use clear, intentional status indicator badges, alert cards, and dynamic warning colors (amber/red) where budgets exceed safe thresholds or cost structures are high-risk.
3. **Simulating Real Impact**: Ensure simulations are interactive and highly visual, immediately showing the difference between baseline historical costs and optimized scenarios.
4. **Data Density with Visual Hierarchy**: Maintain absolute readability across desktop and mobile, ensuring tabular data turns into card-based layouts and long lists turn into tabbed switchers.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
- Contrast ratios of at least 4.5:1 for body copy and 3.0:1 for graphical elements.
- Accessible font scaling and weights (using 'Sarabun' for professional Thai readability, 'Outfit' for numerical visual clarity).
- Full responsiveness on tablet and mobile viewports.
